<summary>
In this lecture, Ralf Jeutter discusses Hahnemann's development of posology in homeopathy. He covers Hahnemann's experimentation with dilutions and succussions to find the optimal dose that is therapeutically effective and safe. Hahnemann started with crude doses and gradually increased dilutions over time, from 1/60 to 1/30 grain to 1/10 million parts. He also introduced the concept of intimate mixing or succussion. Hahnemann found that dry doses were weaker than liquid doses and that the more acute the disease, the higher the potency required. He also distinguished between dose and potency, with dose referring to the amount and frequency of administration and potency referring to the degree of dilution and succussion. Jeutter emphasizes the importance of being flexible and dynamic in potency selection and dosing, and using liquid doses to allow for split dosing and frequent repetition. He also discusses the concept of dynamization, which refers to the process of liberating the spirit-like or energetic potential of the medicinal substance through succussion.
</summary>
