<topics>
1. Pilot studies and feasibility studies: The lecturer explains the purpose and importance of conducting pilot and feasibility studies prior to larger trials to test logistics, gather information, and improve the quality and efficiency of the larger trial.

2. Pragmatic trial design: The lecturer discusses the pragmatic trial design, which evaluates a therapy as it is used in the clinic against usual care and tests overall effectiveness, making it suitable for studying alternative medicine.

3. Guidelines for assessing trials: The lecturer introduces guidelines such as consort, red hot, and Cochrane for assessing the strength of evidence in clinical studies, focusing on aspects such as inclusion/exclusion criteria, randomization, blinding, outcome measures, dropouts, reporting of findings, and statistics.

4. Importance of case studies and observational research: The lecturer emphasizes the relevance of case studies and observational research in understanding new diseases, variations in disease presentation, and exploratory research, particularly in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic.
</topics>
