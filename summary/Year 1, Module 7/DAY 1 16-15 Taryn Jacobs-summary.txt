<summary>
In this lecture, Taryn Jacobs provides a detailed overview of the anatomy and physiology of the heart. She explains the structure of the heart, including the four chambers (left and right atria and ventricles), the septum, and the valves (tricuspid, mitral, and semilunar). She discusses the flow of blood through the heart, distinguishing between oxygenated and deoxygenated blood, and the roles of the pulmonary and systemic circulations. Jacobs also covers the concept of heart murmurs, the coronary arteries, and the importance of the heart's own blood supply. She explains how to take a pulse and what factors can influence heart rate. The lecture also covers the differences between arteries and veins, capillary exchange, blood pressure readings, and the role of the AV node in the heart's conduction system.
</summary>
