<topics>
1. Diabetes insipidus: A condition caused by decreased release or response to antidiuretic hormone (ADH), leading to excessive thirst and urination.

2. Hyperthyroidism: A condition caused by excess production of thyroid hormones, leading to symptoms such as increased heart rate, preference for cold environments, and weight loss. Graves' disease, a subcategory of hyperthyroidism, is an autoimmune condition that can cause additional symptoms such as goiter and eye problems.

3. Hypothyroidism: A condition caused by underproduction of thyroid hormones, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance. Hashimoto's thyroiditis, an autoimmune form of hypothyroidism, is a common cause of this condition.

4. Goiter: An enlargement of the thyroid gland that can be caused by various factors, including iodine deficiency, hyperthyroidism, and hypothyroidism.
</topics>
